How It Works

Fast, explainable analysis of public Twitter/X posts — mapped to a political compass with clear reasoning.

TL;DR: Enter a public Twitter/X handle and hit analyze. We fetch recent public posts, clean the text, extract topic & tone signals, and plot the account on two axes (economic & social). We also show which public figures their language most closely aligns with — plus a short “why.”

Step-by-step

  1. Fetch public tweets. Only public posts — no private data, no paywalled content. We use a recent sample for up-to-date results.
  2. Clean & normalize. Strip links/headers, expand contractions, translate emojis to sentiment so models focus on ideas, not formatting.
  3. Analyze signals. Lightweight models assess sentiment/strength, recurring policy themes (economy, liberty, governance, etc.), and linguistic markers tied to social/economic viewpoints.
  4. Map to the compass. Signals are combined into two axes: economic (left ↔ right) and social (authoritarian ↔ libertarian), then scaled to the grid you see.
  5. Find closest public figures. We compare the account’s linguistic fingerprint to a curated library of public figures and display the closest matches with a quick explanation.
  6. Show context, not judgment. You’ll see the reasoning and examples that influenced the placement so you can judge for yourself.

Privacy & ethics

  • Only public tweets are analyzed — no DMs, no private accounts.
  • No long-term personal data storage; temporary caching is used only for performance.
  • Results are probabilistic. This is for insight and discussion — not targeting or harassment.

Accuracy & limitations

  • Very low-activity accounts produce noisier results.
  • Sarcasm, quotes, and cultural context can shift interpretation.
  • We prioritize clarity and speed over academic-style depth.
